Welcome Address

It is with exceptional fulfilment and gratification that I welcome you to Rural Climate Action (RCA), an initiative on the mission to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, prepare and respond to the effects of climate change through locally based solutions that prepare communities to adjusting to the current and future effects of climate change. We aim to support science based decision–making, promoting practices that are good for the environment, helping communities adapt to climate change, enhance communities’ abilities to withstand climate change effects while moving to renewable energies and aiming to ensure farmers in rural communities adopt to climate smart agriculture for improved food security and reducing food wastage

As Rural Climate Action (RCA), we aim to engage women, men, girls and boys in environmental conservation and climate resilience and adaptation activities. Climate change is not gender neutral and it can amplify existing inequalities of these groups if not properly involved in these interventions.

As a founder and a visionary, I aspire to join hands with everyone who beliefs on locally led climate solutions to address the effects of climate change. Despite of the many challenges we are faced with as a not-for-profit organization, the vision still remains clear in sight and we continuously do our best to make sure these underprivileged communities still get support to adapt and be resilient.

I welcome you on this journey and look forward to your continued support and contribution to this noble course

Who We Are

Rural Climate Action is a national Non-Governmental Organization (NGO) registered in Kenya with reg. no. NGO-VYF25D, Our headquarters is currently located in Lodwar, Turkana County. We are committed to supporting vulnerable communities, particularly children, women, people living with disability, the elderly and the most marginalized to adapt to the impacts of climate change. Our first intended project is to address nutritional diet in primary and secondary schools in Turkana, Isiolo and west Pokot through establishment of school orchards. Fruit trees are a logical, and sweet, extension of school gardens. Not only can they provide an abundant source of nutrient- rich food for students and cafeterias, they can also stand for years as the centerpiece of a living ecology classroom. We aim to support over 50 schools between 2025 and 2030.

The Rural Climate Action (RCA) operational scope includes Turkana, West Pokot and Isiolo counties. RCA was established in 2024 with the primary goal of promoting climate resilience and sustainable livelihoods in rural areas of Kenya.

Our Vision

To see rural communities thrive despite the challenges posed by climate change.

Our Mission

To empower rural communities to adapt to the impacts of climate change and promote sustainable development in rural areas through adaptation and mitigation community based approaches.

Why We Do What We Do

RCA is committed to promoting rural climate change adaptation strategies and resilience, climate advocacy, climate locally led innovations, climate-smart water, sanitation, and hygiene, sustainable practices & ecosystem restoration, conservation and rural policing on climate change. RCA will work with everyone in the affected communities with focus on women, young girls and boys who need to be equipped with the proper resources to transform their own lives and help their entire communities and families, hence readdressing the cause of poverty and climate injustices.
